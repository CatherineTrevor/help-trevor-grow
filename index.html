Welcome to Help Trevor Grow!

Are you age three or older? Would you like to help our friend Trevor grow from an egg, into a caterpillar and finally a butterfly so he can fly away and be with his friends?

If you said yes, then this game is for you!

The fruit that Trevor wants to eat appears, but oh no, it fades away before he has a chance to take a bite! Select the same fruit from the options shown for Trevor to eat - pick the right answer, get a point and start a new turn! Reach 10 points to move onto the next level!

To play, select a level from the options below, or click the Let's Play button to go straight into the game, starting at Egg level. If you find using a mouse hard, maybe try a tablet or phone. Don't forget to pick your colour theme!

Have fun and good luck!

How to play

The game has three levels:

Egg

The simplest level, designed for approx. 3 years old.

Caterpillar

A bit harder, designed for approx. 4 years old.

Butterfly

The hardest level, designed for approx. 5 years old.

These ages are guidance on the level of difficulty involved; they are not an indication of the level a child "should" be. This game is designed to be fun!

The fruit that Trevor wants to eat is shown in the centre of the playing area but slowly disappears before Trevor can eat it. Three options then appear below it. Select the correct option for Trevor to eat, gain a point and start a new turn! Get 10 points to complete a level and start playing the next level.

About

This free-to-play game has been developed to help children from approximately age 3 and up to recognise basic fruits and numbers, with levels that increase slightly in difficulty as they complete each one.

The pop-up when the player selects an incorrect answer is designed to help them understand that they got the answer wrong, but they can try again. Whereas when the correct answer is selected, the big green tick should help them feel good about their achievement and want to continue playing!

Children don’t always get to choose things for themselves, so they have the option to change the colour of the background, giving them some power and control over the game!

If your child has difficulty using a mouse, a tablet or phone might be easier for them.
